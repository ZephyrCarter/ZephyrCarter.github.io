DIY

Here is a list of all the bone and leather tools/implements that I have either made or bought, with the latest at the top

This elegant piece is a deer antler tip I cut off using the serations of an old knife like a saw, cutting it off and using it to produce grooves and serations in stone tools.
This is an elk leather patch used as a guard for my hand when using the antler tip shown above to break small yet sharp pieces of flint or obsidian to create serations.
These two pieces are the source material for the two smaller tools shown above. They also serve a purpose, the deer antler can be used for more tools later on, and the elk hide can be both used for material and as a thigh pad for stricking flint/obsidian, as the shards produced are QUITE sharp.
